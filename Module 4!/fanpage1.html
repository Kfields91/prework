Bio

Canadian singer/songwriter Avril Lavigne's self-empowered female sound has had profound influence on many mainstream artists today like Nicki Minaj, Paramore & Billie Eilish to name a few. Lavigne was born on September 27, 1984 in Belleville, Ontario, Canada and later raised in the much smaller Greater Napenee, Ontario. Having a talent for singing from the age of 2, Avril soon begain performing at country fairs covering counrty music. Her earliest influences were Garth Brooks, Shania Twain, and the Dixie Chicks. As she grew her taste music influence expanded to Janis Joplin, Alanis Morrisette, Marilyn Manson, Nirvana, The Used, No doubt among others.


Avril's first album with her Debut song "Sk8er Boi" brought her to the pop/punk stage. Many include Avril as one of the first major influences in this newer genre sounds including emo. She's often referred to as "The Princess of Punk". Her first album Let Go reached number 2 on the Billboard top 200 album charts.


In 2014 Avril Lavigne was diagnosed with Lyme Disease inspiring her to write her newest album Head Above Water. She has taken to giving back and the link to The Avril Lavigne Foundation can be found on her official fansite www.avrillavigne.com. Her foundation has raised money for different charities such as the Easter Seals, Global Lyme Awareness, LymeLight Foundation, Make-A-Wish, UNICIF, and Race to Erase MS including many others.
